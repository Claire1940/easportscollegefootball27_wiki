<Section icon={Swords} title="On-Field Domination: Custom Adjustments & Macros">

Once you have built an elite roster using the **ea sports college football 27 rainmaker** archetype, you must pilot them effectively on game day [1, 2]. The revamped Custom Adjustments system acts as an extension of your playbook, allowing you to create custom on-field checks that bypass standard pre-play limitations [1]. In real-life football, defensive coordinators do not change the entire play when an offense motions; they make localized checks [1]. Custom Adjustments replicate this exact behavior [1].

For example, if you come out in a base match coverage and the offense motions a receiver to create a trips formation, you can execute a pre-saved check (like rolling your safety down and locking the backside cornerback in man coverage) instantly [1].

### Preset vs. Custom Macros

If you prefer not to build custom systems from scratch, the game provides highly effective preset macros that can be deployed immediately on both sides of the ball [1].

| Adjustment Type | Defensive Preset | Offensive Preset | Tactical Advantage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Coverage Shading** | Play Short Routes | Mesh Out | Shuts down quick checkdowns or creates immediate space over the middle [1] |
| **Deep Protection** | No Deep Passes | Go Vertical | Prevents explosive chunk plays or forces a deep safety conflict [1] |
| **Run Commitment** | Play Inside Run | Smart Routes | Pinches defensive linemen into gaps or adjusts route depths to the first-down marker [1, 2] |
| **Containment** | QB Scramble | All Verticals | Sacrifices a coverage defender to spy highly mobile quarterbacks [1] |

To access these adjustments during a game, simply hold the corresponding play button at the play call screen to view your saved macros [1]. Once at the line of scrimmage, tap **L1 (PS5) or LB (Xbox)** and select your desired adjustment [1]. What used to take 15 to 20 rapid button presses is now executed in a fraction of a second, keeping your defense aligned and ready before the snap [1].

<Callout type="warning" title="Anti-Spam Balancing">
To maintain realistic game balance, you cannot endlessly stack adjustments while players are in motion [1]. Players must fully set at the line of scrimmage before a second macro adjustment can be successfully applied [1].
</Callout>
