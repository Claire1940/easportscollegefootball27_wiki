<Section icon={Shield} title="Activating and Configuring Coach Mode Settings">

To get the most out of **ea sports college football 27 coach mode**, you must properly configure your game settings. These options are found within the main settings menu and can be toggled before starting a Dynasty or an exhibition match. Enabling these features ensures the AI takes over the mechanical burden, leaving you to handle the high-level decision-making.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access Gameplay Settings">
    Navigate to the Main Menu or the Dynasty Hub. Enter the 'Settings' tab and select 'Gameplay.'
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enable Coach Mode Toggle">
    Locate the 'Coach Mode' toggle. When enabled, this ensures that the CPU will handle passing and running once you have called the play and snapped the ball.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Configure Automation Options">
    Set 'Auto Quarterback' and 'Auto Snap' to 'On.' This prevents the game from waiting for your button input to throw or hike, creating a seamless sideline experience.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Set Communication Difficulty">
    Adjust the 'Pre-Play Audio' and 'Communication Window' settings to match your desired challenge level. Remember that the 15-second lockout is a hard rule in competitive environments.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Notice">
Even in Coach Mode, you are responsible for the snap if 'Auto Snap' is off. If you are spending too much time on custom adjustments, you risk a 'Delay of Game' penalty once the 15-second communication window closes.

### The Communication Window
The most significant change in this year's Coach Mode is the 15-second lockout. Once the play clock hits 15, the "sideline" is effectively cut off from the players. You can no longer call audibles, change hot routes, or shift protections. This forces you to make your reads and adjustments quickly as soon as the players break the huddle.

| Mechanic | Control Input | Resulting Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Read** | Hold RT/R2 + Receiver Icon | QB prioritizes this target in their progression |
| **RPO Coaching** | Directional Input during snap | Tells QB to prioritize the Hand-off or the Throw |
| **Coach Vision** | Left D-Pad | Opens HUD to see player matchups and fatigue |
| **Dynamic Subs** | Right D-Pad | Quickly swap tired players for fresh backups |
| **Custom Adjustments** | L1/LB | Applies saved macro packages (e.g., "Defend Screen") |

<Callout type="success" title="Tactical Advantage">

To truly dominate in **ea sports college football 27 coach mode**, you must move beyond basic play-calling and utilize the **Formation Shifts** and **Custom Adjustments** systems. These tools allow you to disguise your intent and force the opposing defense (or offense) into unfavorable matchups.

### Formation Shifts
Formation shifts allow you to break the huddle in one look and shift to another while keeping the same personnel on the field. This is vital in Coach Mode for identifying if the defense is in man or zone coverage.

1. **Select Base Play**: Choose a play from a standard personnel grouping (e.g., 11 Personnel).
2. **Toggle Shift**: Use the play-call screen to toggle a shift to a different formation within that grouping.
3. **Analyze Reaction**: Watch how the defenders move during the shift. If a cornerback follows a receiver across the formation, it’s likely man coverage.
4. **Finalize Read**: Use the remaining time before the 15-second lockout to audible if the shift reveals a disadvantage.

| Tactical Tool | Primary Use | Coach Mode Synergy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Tackle Stick** | Setting aggressive vs. safe tackling | Reduces big plays when AI is in control |
| **QB Sneak Meter** | Managing short-yardage pushes | Relies on trench ratings and timing |
| **Wear and Tear** | Managing player fatigue | Prevents AI errors caused by exhausted starters |
