<Section icon={Target} title="The Dynasty Blueprint: Budgeting for Success">

The **Dynasty Blueprint** is your program's annual financial plan. Every year, you earn **Dynasty Points** that must be distributed across four key pillars: Coaching Staff, Facilities, Recruiting NIL, and Roster NIL. Success in **ea sports college football 27 dynasty mode** requires a strategic allocation that matches your school's identity. For example, a school with a lower prestige rating might need to over-invest in facilities to improve player progression, while a top-tier program might focus heavily on Roster NIL to prevent stars from entering the Transfer Portal.

| Pillar | Focus Area | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Coaching Staff** | Coordinators / Support | On-field boosts and scouting speed |
| **Facilities** | Academic / Athletic | Player progression and recruiting grades |
| **Recruiting NIL** | Prospect Incentives | Weekly influence bonus for recruits |
| **Roster NIL** | Retention / Morale | Reduces transfer risk for current stars |

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategy Tip">
While the CPU can manage your Blueprint via Auto Settings, manual allocation allows you to target specific weaknesses, such as a failing weight room or a lack of recruiting presence in high-talent states.
</Callout>

Building a staff is the first step in the preseason. You can hire up to five support staff slots, depending on your **Team Prestige**. These roles are specialized, helping with everything from injury prevention to social media branding, which indirectly boosts your NIL appeal.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Recruiting and NIL Strategy in 2026">

Recruiting in **ea sports college football 27 dynasty mode** has moved beyond simple phone calls. The introduction of **Recruiting NIL** means every scholarship offer is a financial negotiation. Players now have an "Expected NIL Amount," and offering up to twice that value can provide a significant weekly influence bonus. However, spending too much on one prospect might leave your budget thin for the rest of your class.
