<Section icon={Target} title="Dynasty Blueprint and Program Identity">

In **ea sports college football 27 dynasty deep dive**, the core of your journey begins with the **Dynasty Blueprint**. This system replaces static progression with a dynamic annual budget. Every year, your program is awarded **Dynasty Points** based on performance, school prestige, and fan engagement. These points are the lifeblood of your program, requiring careful allocation across coaching, facilities, and NIL (Name, Image, and Likeness) funds.

Before you even spend a single point, you must align your strategy with **Athletic Director (AD) Expectations**. These are school-specific goals that define how you are evaluated. Some schools prioritize winning a conference championship immediately, while others focus on building a recruiting pipeline in specific territories or maintaining a high academic standard for the roster.

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategy Selection">
When starting your Dynasty, check the **Patience** meter of your Athletic Director. Schools with low patience will fire you quickly if you miss early win totals, while developmental programs offer more time to build through recruiting.
</Callout>

| Program Type | Primary Focus | AD Patience | Dynasty Point Multiplier |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **National Powerhouse** | Playoff/Championship | Low | High (1.5x) |
| **Rising Contender** | Top 25/Bowl Wins | Medium | Standard (1.0x) |
| **Rebuilding Project** | Roster Foundation | High | Low (0.8x) |
| **Recruiting Specialist** | Pipeline Depth | Medium | Standard (1.0x) |

The **Blueprint Strategy** interface allows you to set a suggested allocation for your resources. While you can manually override these at any time, setting a clear direction helps the AI assist you in "Auto" modes. For example, a **Developmental Blueprint** will lean heavily into facilities to boost player XP gains, whereas a **Mercenary Blueprint** might dump 80% of funds into NIL to lure top-tier transfers.

</Section>

<Section icon={Building} title="Facilities and Staff Infrastructure">

Facilities in **EA Sports College Football 27** are no longer just a static grade on a recruiting screen. They are a long-term investment that requires annual maintenance. Upgrading your facilities increases your **Athletic Facilities grade**, which provides massive bonuses to player progression and reduces the "Wear and Tear" effects during the season.

<Callout type="info" title="Facility Tiers">
Facility tiers range from **Basic Facility (F)** to **National Powerhouse (A+)**. Higher tiers unlock more **Equipment Slots**, allowing you to equip specialized items that provide specific buffs to your team.
</Callout>

| Facility Tier | Grade | Equipment Slots | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic** | F to D- | 1 | Minimum upkeep cost |
| **Standard** | D to C+ | 2 | Balanced progression |
| **Advanced** | B- to B+ | 3 | Reduced practice injury chance |
| **Elite** | A- to A | 4 | Significant XP boost for all players |
| **Powerhouse** | A+ | 5 | Max progression and NIL attraction |

Staff management has also seen a significant overhaul. You are responsible for hiring not just coordinators, but a full support staff. These roles are tied to your **Team Prestige**. As your program grows, you unlock up to five support staff slots. These staffers provide "behind-the-scenes" bonuses, such as improved scouting speed, better injury recovery, or increased influence in the Transfer Portal.

<Section icon={UserPlus} title="Modern Recruiting and NIL Strategy">

Recruiting is the most intensive part of any **ea sports college football 27 dynasty deep dive**. The process begins in **Week 0**, where scholarship offers are officially extended. Unlike previous years, these offers now include an **NIL component**. Every prospect has an "Expected NIL" value. To gain a massive influence bonus, you can offer up to **two times** the prospect's expected amount, but doing so drains your limited NIL budget.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Scouting">
    Spend your early hours identifying prospects that fit your scheme. Look for players in your **Primary Pipelines** for a natural influence boost.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Scholarship Offer">
    Extend offers in Week 0. Decide whether to offer a standard scholarship or a **NIL-enhanced package** to jump-start the interest meter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Weekly Influence">
    Allocate your weekly hours to "Pitch," "Visit," or "Social Media" actions. The efficiency of these actions is determined by your **Recruiting NIL** budget.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Closing the Deal">
    Once a player reaches their "Top 3" or "Top 5" stage, use **Hard Sells** to align your program's strengths with their specific motivations (e.g., Playing Time, Pro Factory).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="NIL Risk">
Overspending on a single 5-star recruit can leave you with zero funds to retain your current starters during the **Roster Retention** phase at the end of the year. Balance is key.
</Callout>

| Recruiting Action | Hourly Cost | Influence Level | NIL Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Send House Call** | 50 | Massive | High |
| **Hard Sell** | 40 | High | Medium |
| **Soft Sell** | 20 | Medium | Low |
| **Social Media** | 5 | Low | None |

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Roster Retention and the Transfer Portal">

Building a team is one thing; keeping it together is another. At the end of every season, you enter the **End of Season Recap**. This is where you must manage **Roster NIL**. Players on your current roster may consider entering the Transfer Portal or declaring for the NFL Draft based on their **Draft Stock** and **Legacy Score**.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Retention Strategy">
    Use your remaining Blueprint funds to increase NIL for star players. This reduces the risk of them transferring to rival programs for "bigger bags."
  </Card>
  <Card title="Portal Hunting">
    The Transfer Portal moves fast. Focus your NIL offers on "Instant Starters" who fill immediate holes left by graduating seniors or draft departures.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Draft Persuasion">
    For players with a 1st or 2nd round projection, you can attempt to persuade them to stay for one more year. This requires a high **Leadership** rating from your coach.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Scholarship Management">
    You must stay under the 85-man scholarship limit. Use the recap phase to encourage underperforming players to move on, freeing up space for new recruits.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="success" title="Retention Tip">
Players with high **Loyalty** traits are less likely to transfer even if they receive better NIL offers elsewhere. Prioritize these traits during high school recruiting to ensure long-term stability.
</Callout>

</Section>
