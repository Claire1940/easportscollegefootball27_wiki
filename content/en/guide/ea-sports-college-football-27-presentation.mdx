Weather is no longer a static backdrop; it is a dynamic gameplay mechanic that evolves over the course of four quarters. A sunny afternoon kickoff can slowly transition into a heavy rainstorm by the second half, altering both the visual aesthetic and the mechanical physics of the football.

| Weather Condition | Visual Changes | Gameplay Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Light Rain** | Wet jersey textures | Minor reduction in ball handling security |
| **Heavy Snow** | Accumulation on turf and lines | High slip frequency, difficult pass catching |
| **Muddy Turf** | Severe uniform degradation | Slower player acceleration, difficult kicking game |

As precipitation intensifies, players will experience authentic uniform degradation. White jerseys accumulate grass stains, mud, and turf paint, while the field itself shows visible wear in high-traffic areas like the red zone and between the hashes.
