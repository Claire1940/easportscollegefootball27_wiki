<Section icon={Target} title="Dynasty Mode: Building Your Program Blueprint">

Dynasty mode has been redesigned around the "Program Identity" concept. You are no longer just calling plays; you are managing a multi-million dollar athletic department. The new **Dynasty Blueprint** system forces you to allocate a limited budget across three core pillars: Coaching Staff, Facilities, and NIL (Name, Image, and Likeness).

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Accept AD Expectations">
    Every program has specific goals set by the Athletic Director. Some schools prioritize winning championships immediately, while others focus on recruiting pipelines or academic stability. Failing these can lead to a quick termination.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Allocate Dynasty Points">
    Your annual budget is distributed via Dynasty Points. You must decide if you want to invest in a 5-star coaching staff, upgrade your training facilities to A+ status, or dump resources into the NIL fund to attract top-tier transfers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Preseason Scouting">
    Scholarship offers begin in Week 0. Use this time to identify prospects that fit your scheme. Remember, high-rated recruits now expect higher NIL payouts, which can drain your Blueprint budget if you aren't careful.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Roster Retention">
    At the end of the season, you must use Roster NIL to keep your stars from entering the Transfer Portal. A player's "Risk of Leaving" meter is directly influenced by the financial support and playing time you provide.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Recruiting Strategy">
Focus on your "Pipeline" states first. Recruiting players from your home territory costs fewer Dynasty Points and grants a significant bonus to your pitch effectiveness.
</Callout>

</Section>
