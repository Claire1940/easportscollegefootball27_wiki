<Section icon={Target} title="Dynasty Blueprint & Recruiting Strategy">

Dynasty Mode in College Football 27 is built around program identity, annual budgets, coaching staff, facilities, NIL, and a deep recruiting system [1, 3]. Rather than simply chasing five-star recruits, you must manage your program using the Dynasty Blueprint [1, 3]. Every year, your Athletic Director sets expectations, and your program is awarded Dynasty Points as an annual budget [3]. 

To build a sustainable powerhouse, follow this step-by-step strategy to allocate your resources and secure top-tier talent.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze Athletic Director Expectations">
    Every Dynasty job has school-specific expectations shaped by program priorities, fan patience, and school identity [3]. Choose a program that fits your playstyle—whether it is a win-now powerhouse or a long-term rebuild like North Dakota State or Sacramento State, which join the FBS ranks to bring the total to 138 playable programs [3]. AD goals will dictate how much patience the board has with your recruiting and on-field results.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Allocate the Dynasty Blueprint Budget">
    Your annual Dynasty Points are split across coaching staff, facilities, and NIL [3]. Do not spend blindly. A balanced approach is recommended: allocate 30% to Coaching Staff, 35% to Facilities, 20% to Recruiting NIL, and 15% to Roster NIL. You can adjust this blueprint strategy manually or use auto settings to let the CPU manage specific allocations [3].
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Hire and Upgrade Your Coaching Staff">
    Head coaches can invest Dynasty Points into coordinators and support staff [3]. Support staff slots are tied to your Team Prestige, allowing up to five slots for top-tier programs [3]. Note that support staff can only be hired and fired during the preseason [3]. Upgrading your coordinators unlocks unique recruiting pipelines and on-field boosts for your players.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Scout and Deploy Recruiting NIL">
    Preseason is for scouting, while scholarship offers begin in Week 0 [3]. Because offers now include NIL, recruiting is highly competitive. You can offer up to two times a prospect’s expected NIL amount to earn the maximum weekly influence bonus [3]. Focus your recruiting NIL on positions of need and players within your pipeline states to maximize your points.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Manage Roster NIL and Retention">
    During the End of Season Recap, you must allocate Roster NIL to retain players already in your program [3]. Increasing a player’s NIL allocation reduces their risk of entering the Transfer Portal or leaving early for the draft [3]. Conversely, reducing their NIL to free up budget space increases their likelihood of transferring out [3].
  </Step>
</Steps>
