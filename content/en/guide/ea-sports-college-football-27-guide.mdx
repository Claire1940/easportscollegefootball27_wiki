<Section icon={Target} title="Dynasty Blueprint and Recruiting Strategy">

Dynasty Mode has been rebuilt around program identity, annual resource allocation, and realistic roster management. Your journey begins with selecting a school based on Athletic Director Expectations, which dictate your job security. The program count has expanded to 138 with the inclusion of North Dakota State and Sacramento State.

Managing your program requires balancing your yearly budget using the Dynasty Blueprint. Follow these structured steps to establish a winning program.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Allocate the Dynasty Blueprint Budget">
    Each offseason, you receive Dynasty Points representing your program's annual budget. You must manually divide these points among three core pillars: Coaching Staff, Facilities, and NIL. Setting a Blueprint Strategy helps guide the CPU if you choose to automate parts of the process, but manual allocation is highly recommended for competitive runs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Hire and Manage Support Staff">
    Preseason is the only window available to hire or fire coordinators and support staff. Your program's Team Prestige determines your available support staff slots, capping out at five slots. High-tier support staff provide passive boosts to player progression and recruiting efficiency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Initiate Scouting and Week 0 Offers">
    Use the early weeks to scout prospects. Once Week 0 arrives, you can officially extend scholarship offers. In this edition, scholarship offers are tied directly to recruiting NIL. You can offer up to two times a prospect's expected NIL amount to secure the maximum weekly influence bonus.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Roster Retention and NIL Management">
    During the End of Season Recap, you must allocate Roster NIL to keep your current players from entering the Transfer Portal or declaring early for the draft. Increasing a player's NIL share reduces their transfer risk, while neglecting their demands will almost certainly push them to test the market.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Invest in Long-Term Facility Upgrades">
    Do not neglect your Athletic Facilities grade. Upgrading facilities from a Basic Facility (F) to a National Powerhouse (A+) increases your equipment slots from one to five. Quality equipment reduces practice Wear and Tear, lowers injury risks, and accelerates player attribute progression over multiple seasons.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Recruiting Tip">
Transfer portal players place a much heavier emphasis on immediate NIL offers compared to high school recruits. Because their transfer recruiting window is incredibly short, saving a reserve portion of your Dynasty Points specifically for portal NIL is a vital strategy.
</Callout>

</Section>

On-field execution in College Football 27 demands quick diagnostic skills and precise execution. The pre-play interface has been streamlined, grouping protection and coverage controls into unified hubs. Defensive playbooks have expanded significantly, featuring 31 distinct playbooks and 22 brand-new formations, including the 3-3-5 3 High Over and Nickel Double Mug looks.

To help you master the new mechanics, we have outlined the expanded Tackle Stick controls and pre-play defensive adjustments.

| Right Stick Direction | Tackle Type | Best Defensive Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Up | Hit Stick | Forcing fumbles on stationary or fatigued ball-carriers |

| Left | Lunge Tackle | Chasing outside runs and stopping sideline scrambles |
| Right | Wrap Tackle | Secure, conservative finishes to prevent broken tackles |

| QB Sneak Meter | Pre-Snap | Evaluates offensive line push against defensive tackle shed before the snap |

<Callout type="tip" title="Timing-Based Catching">
When throwing downfield, you can hold the catch button (Aggressive, Possession, or RAC) and release it within the green timing window. Perfect timing guarantees a clean catch attempt, whereas a poor release leaves the outcome entirely dependent on your receiver's base ratings and defender contact.
</Callout>

For players who prefer a strategic, managerial approach over stick-skills, Coach Mode can be enabled in the settings. This automates post-snap execution while leaving play-calling, RPO decisions, and primary receiver designations in your hands.
