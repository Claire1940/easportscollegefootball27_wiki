<Section icon={Target} title="Physique Customization and Max Potential Allocation">

Once your position is selected, you must build your athlete’s physical profile [1]. The game uses a dynamically connected physique system where height, weight, and body type are directly tied to your attribute limits [1]. A taller, heavier build naturally yields higher caps in strength and power, while a lighter build grants access to superior speed, acceleration, and agility [1].

Understanding the difference between physical limits and developmental ceilings is crucial:

1. **Physique Caps**: The absolute physical limit an attribute can reach based on your height, weight, and body type [1].
2. **Max Potential**: The actual developmental ceiling you allocate using your starting potential points [1]. If your physique allows for 95 Speed but you only allocate points up to 92, then 92 becomes your hard cap [1].

| Attribute Range | Upgrade Cost | Investment Priority | Developmental Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Base (25 to 60)** | Very Low | Low | Establishes baseline competency |
| **Mid-Tier (61 to 80)** | Moderate | Medium | Balanced growth across secondary stats |
| **Elite (81 to 90+)** | High | High | Unlocks elite physical and mental abilities |

If you prefer to skip building a player from scratch, you can choose a **Legend Template** [1]. These preset builds are inspired by college football legends like Reggie Bush, Vince Young, and Tim Tebow, offering balanced, recognizable playstyles right out of the gate [1].

<Callout type="tip" title="Max Potential Strategy">
Be careful when upgrading attributes; certain ratings are linked together to maintain positional balance [1]. For example, raising a quarterback's Short Throw Accuracy past certain thresholds will automatically pull up Medium and Deep Accuracy to keep development realistic [1].
</Callout>
