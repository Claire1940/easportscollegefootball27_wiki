<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Competitive Tiers and Point Scaling Rules">

To ensure balanced matchmaking and reward skilled execution, the competitive online modes utilize a tiered ranking system. The competitive point system rewards risk. If you choose a lower-tier team and successfully pull off an upset against a Tier 1 opponent, you will earn significantly more ranking points. Road wins also carry a multiplier, making a successful campaign in a hostile stadium the fastest way to climb the competitive ladder from Recruit to Heisman division.

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Rebuilding Programs in Dynasty">

Rebuilding a program in Dynasty Mode requires long-term planning, strategic resource allocation, and a deep understanding of the Dynasty Blueprint. You cannot simply rely on on-field performance; you must manage coaching staff, upgrade athletic facilities, and navigate the complex landscape of player recruitment and NIL deals.

Follow this systematic progression to transform a struggling Tier 5 school into a national powerhouse capable of competing with the highest-rated programs.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze Athletic Director Expectations">
    Every program has unique expectations set by the administration. Assess whether your goals focus on immediate wins, long-term roster development, or regional recruiting dominance before spending your budget.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Allocate the Dynasty Blueprint Budget">
    Your program receives an annual budget of Dynasty Points. Divide these points strategically across coaching staff salaries, facility upgrades, recruiting NIL, and roster retention NIL based on your immediate needs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Assemble Your Coaching Staff">
    Hire coordinators who complement your playbook and recruit support staff during the preseason. Support staff slots are locked once the season starts, and their abilities directly impact player progression and scouting efficiency.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Scout and Secure Recruits with NIL">
    Begin offering scholarships in Week 0. Use your recruiting NIL budget to target high-potential prospects in your pipeline states. Offering up to twice a recruit's expected NIL value provides the highest weekly influence bonus.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Manage Roster Retention">
    During the End of Season Recap, allocate roster NIL to key players who are considering the Transfer Portal or entering the draft early. Properly balanced retention budgets prevent sudden talent drains.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Staff Management">
Support staff slots are directly tied to your program's Prestige level. A basic program starts with fewer slots, while a prestigious school can unlock up to five support staff slots to accelerate player development.
</Callout>
